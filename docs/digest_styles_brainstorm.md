# Digest Style Options: Rethinking the Approach

## Current Problem
The existing 3 styles (Concise, Detailed, Bullet Points) feel generic and don't clearly differentiate value or use case.

## Key Questions to Answer
1. **What's the core value?** Aggregation + AI summarization
2. **What do users actually need?** Quick scan vs. deep understanding vs. reference
3. **What fits minimalistic design?** Less is more, but what's the "more"?

---

## Alternative Approach 1: Information Density

### Style 1: **Headlines Only**
**Target:** Ultra-busy users who just want to know what happened
- Newsletter name
- Subject lines only (no summaries)
- Minimal formatting
- **Example:**
  ```
  The Morning Brew
  • Fed Signals Rate Cuts Ahead
  • Tech Earnings Preview
  • Market Outlook: Bullish Signals
  ```

### Style 2: **Summary + Headlines**
**Target:** Users who want context but stay scannable
- Newsletter name
- 2-3 sentence AI summary
- Subject lines listed below
- **Example:**
  ```
  The Morning Brew
  Markets rallied 2% on Fed policy news, with tech stocks leading. 
  Q4 earnings expectations are bullish.
  
  • Fed Signals Rate Cuts Ahead
  • Tech Earnings Preview
  • Market Outlook: Bullish Signals
  ```

### Style 3: **Full Summary**
**Target:** Users who want comprehensive understanding
- Newsletter name
- 4-6 sentence AI summary (covers all key points)
- No subject lines (summary is comprehensive)
- **Example:**
  ```
  The Morning Brew
  Today's newsletter focuses on the Federal Reserve's latest policy 
  announcement, which sent markets up 2% in early trading. Tech 
  stocks led the rally, with Apple and Microsoft both gaining over 
  3%. The newsletter analyzes Q4 earnings expectations, noting 
  strong signals from major tech companies. Market outlook remains 
  bullish for the coming week, with analysts predicting continued 
  growth driven by AI investments and consumer spending resilience.
  ```

**Pros:** Clear progression (minimal → balanced → comprehensive)
**Cons:** Still feels a bit generic

## Alternative Approach 2: Use Case Based

### Style 1: **Morning Brief**
**Target:** Quick morning scan before work
- Newsletter name
- 1-sentence summary per newsletter
- Top 3 subject lines
- **Example:**
  ```
  The Morning Brew
  Markets up 2% on Fed news; tech stocks rally.
  • Fed Signals Rate Cuts Ahead
  • Tech Earnings Preview
  • Market Outlook: Bullish Signals
  ```

### Style 2: **Deep Read**
**Target:** Users who want comprehensive understanding
- Newsletter name
- Full AI summary (4-6 sentences)
- All subject lines with brief context
- **Example:**
  ```
  The Morning Brew
  [Full 4-6 sentence summary covering all key points]
  
  Articles:
  • Fed Signals Rate Cuts Ahead - Analysis of policy implications
  • Tech Earnings Preview - What to watch this quarter
  • Market Outlook: Bullish Signals - Expert predictions
  ```

### Style 3: **Reference Mode**
**Target:** Users who want structured info for later review
- Newsletter name
- Summary in structured format (key points extracted)
- Subject lines organized by topic/theme
- **Example:**
  ```
  The Morning Brew
  
  Key Points:
  • Markets: +2% on Fed policy news
  • Tech: Apple +3%, Microsoft +3%
  • Outlook: Bullish for Q4
  
  Articles by Topic:
  Policy: Fed Signals Rate Cuts Ahead
  Earnings: Tech Earnings Preview
  Analysis: Market Outlook: Bullish Signals
  ```

**Pros:** Clear use cases, feels more purposeful
**Cons:** Might be too prescriptive


## ✅ DECISION: Use Case Based Approach

### Style 1: **Morning Brief**
**Target:** Quick morning scan before work
- Newsletter name
- 1-sentence summary per newsletter
- Top 3 subject lines
- **Use case:** Morning coffee, quick check-in
- **Example:**
  ```
  The Morning Brew
  Markets up 2% on Fed news; tech stocks rally.
  • Fed Signals Rate Cuts Ahead
  • Tech Earnings Preview
  • Market Outlook: Bullish Signals
  ```

### Style 2: **Deep Read**
**Target:** Users who want comprehensive understanding
- Newsletter name
- Full AI summary (4-6 sentences)
- All subject lines with brief context
- **Use case:** Evening reading, deep understanding
- **Example:**
  ```
  The Morning Brew
  Today's newsletter covers the Federal Reserve's latest policy 
  announcement, which sent markets up 2% in early trading. Tech 
  stocks led the rally, with Apple and Microsoft both gaining over 
  3%. The newsletter analyzes Q4 earnings expectations, noting 
  strong signals from major tech companies. Market outlook remains 
  bullish for the coming week.
  
  Articles:
  • Fed Signals Rate Cuts Ahead - Analysis of policy implications
  • Tech Earnings Preview - What to watch this quarter
  • Market Outlook: Bullish Signals - Expert predictions
  ```

### Style 3: **Reference Mode**
**Target:** Users who want structured info for later review
- Newsletter name
- Summary in structured format (key points extracted)
- Subject lines organized by topic/theme
- **Use case:** Reference, later review, note-taking
- **Example:**
  ```
  The Morning Brew
  
  Key Points:
  • Markets: +2% on Fed policy news
  • Tech: Apple +3%, Microsoft +3%
  • Outlook: Bullish for Q4
  
  Articles by Topic:
  Policy: Fed Signals Rate Cuts Ahead
  Earnings: Tech Earnings Preview
  Analysis: Market Outlook: Bullish Signals
  ```

**Why this works:**
- Clear use cases tied to when/how users consume content
- Feels purposeful, not generic
- Fits minimalistic design (each style is clean)
- Names are intuitive and memorable

## Settings & Configuration

### Onboarding Flow
- **Default:** Show 3 style options (Morning Brief, Deep Read, Reference Mode)
- **User selects:** One style during onboarding
- **Stored in:** `digest_configs.style` column

### Settings Page (Post-Onboarding)
- **Changeable:** Users can change digest style anytime in Settings
- **Preview:** Show preview of selected style before saving
- **Default:** Keep current selection, allow switching

### Future: Advanced Styles (Settings Only)
- **Location:** Settings → Digest Preferences → Advanced Styles
- **Purpose:** Offer niche/experimental styles without cluttering onboarding
- **Implementation:** Build out later (see Future Styles list below)
